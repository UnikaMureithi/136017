Information about the CVD Prediction Application

Welcome to the Cardiovascular Disease (CVD) Prediction Web Application!
Our tool utilizes a variety of features to assess the risk of cardiovascular disease.
These features are categorized into objective and subjective aspects, each contributing
valuable information to the prediction model.

Objective Features

1. Age:
Data Type: Integer (years)
Input Variable: age

2. Height:
Data Type: Integer (cm)
Input Variable: height

3. Weight:
Data Type: Float (kg)
Input Variable: weight

4. Gender:
Data Type: Categorical Code- Male=0 and Female=1
Input Variable: gender

Examination Features

(For the features below, as a Patient, one may be required to visit a clinic in order to get proper accurate readings)

1. Systolic Blood Pressure:
Data Type: Integer
Input Variable: ap_hi
Description: Systolic blood pressure is the higher of the two numbers measured when your heart beats.

2. Diastolic Blood Pressure:
Data Type: Integer
Input Variable: ap_lo
Description: Diastolic blood pressure is the lower of the two numbers measured when your heart is at rest between beats.

Blood Pressure Categories

Blood Pressure Category Systolic mm Hg (upper number) Diastolic mm Hg (lower number)
NORMAL LESS THAN 120 LESS THAN 80
ELEVATED 120 – 129 LESS THAN 80
HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE (HYPERTENSION) STAGE 1 130 – 139 80 – 89
HIGH BLOOD PRESSURE (HYPERTENSION) STAGE 2 140 OR HIGHER 90 OR HIGHER
HYPERTENSIVE CRISIS (consult your doctor immediately) HIGHER THAN 180 HIGHER THAN 120

3. Cholesterol:
Data Type: Categorical (1: Normal, 2: Above Normal, 3: Well Above Normal)
Input Variable: cholesterol
Description: Cholesterol levels indicate the amount of fatty deposits in the blood. A simple blood test called a lipid panel is used to measure total cholesterol

4. Glucose:
Data Type: Categorical (1: Normal, 2: Above Normal, 3: Well Above Normal)
Input Variable: gluc
Description: Glucose levels indicate the amount of sugar in the blood. A simple blood test can be used to measure glucose
